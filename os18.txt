🔹 7. C-SCAN Algorithm Logic

👉 C-SCAN = Circular SCAN

Moves in one direction only
After reaching end → jumps to other end
Continues in same direction
🔹 8. RIGHT Direction (dir == 1)
➤ Step 1: Move Right
if(req[i] >= start)
Serve all requests on the right
total += abs(head - req[i]);
head = req[i];
➤ Step 2: Move to Disk End
printf("%d\t%d\n", size-1, abs(head - (size-1)));
Move to last track (size - 1)
➤ Step 3: Jump to Start (0)
head = 0;
printf("0\t%d\n", size-1);
total += size-1;

👉 Important:

This is circular jump
Movement counted = full disk length
➤ Step 4: Continue Remaining Requests
if(req[i] < start)
Serve left-side requests
🔹 9. LEFT Direction (dir == 0)
➤ Step 1: Move Left
if(req[i] <= start)
Serve left-side requests
➤ Step 2: Move to Start (0)
printf("0\t%d\n", abs(head - 0));
total += abs(head);
➤ Step 3: Jump to End
head = size - 1;
printf("%d\t%d\n", size-1, size-1);
total += size-1;

👉 Circular jump to highest track
